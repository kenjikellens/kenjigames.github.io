About KenjiGames

The Mission & Direction

From gaming roots to open-source media tools, ethical software, and public-interest projects.

Our Story

KenjiGames began with gaming: experiments, creative worlds, media, and the kind of projects that come from wanting to build something fun instead of waiting for permission. That origin still matters. The name stayed because it represents curiosity, independence, and the habit of turning ideas into real things.

Over time, the mission grew beyond games. KenjiGames became a place for open-source tools, ethical alternatives, media players, research archives, and practical utilities that respect the user more than the platform. The goal is not just to make software look polished. The goal is to build projects that feel useful, transparent, and worth trusting.

That is why projects like IVIDS, IVIDS Music, and The Great Awakening sit next to each other here. They may look different on the surface, but they share the same instinct: remove unnecessary control, reduce noise, and give people a clearer way to watch, listen, organize, question, or explore.

The studio is still small and experimental, but that is part of the point. KenjiGames can move between entertainment, utilities, research, and media without needing every project to fit inside one corporate category. If an idea helps people understand more, own more, or depend less on hostile platforms, it belongs here.

Independent By Nature

KenjiGames is built around independent thinking: make the thing, question the default, publish what can be shared, and keep the user at the center.

Our Core Pillars

The values guiding our projects, from entertainment software to public-interest research.

Respect The User

A good tool should not fight the person using it. We care about clean interfaces, fewer distractions, less advertising pressure, and software that helps people do what they came to do.

Open Source Matters

Open-source projects invite inspection and improvement. When possible, KenjiGames publishes code so users can learn from it, challenge it, adapt it, or build something better from it.

Public-Interest Curiosity

Some projects are about comfort, and some are about questions. The Great Awakening exists because information, power, institutions, and public narratives deserve careful investigation.

What KenjiGames Is Becoming

KenjiGames is becoming a small ecosystem of projects that share one direction: more user control, more transparency, and less dependence on closed, ad-driven, or manipulative platforms. IVIDS is part of that mission because it imagines streaming and Live TV as something cleaner and more unified. IVIDS Music follows the same idea for listening. Music File Organiser helps people take back control of messy local libraries. The Great Awakening gives public-interest research a dedicated home.

We still care about design, speed, and polish, but those are not the whole story. A beautiful interface means very little if it only exists to trap attention, collect data, or hide how things work. The better path is software that feels good to use while staying honest about its purpose.

That is the thread connecting the work here: independent media tools, open repositories, practical utilities, and projects that encourage people to look closer. KenjiGames may have started with games, but its future is broader: build useful things, keep them understandable, and make them available to people who want alternatives.

Why Open And Ethical?

Modern digital life is full of tradeoffs people never clearly agreed to. Streaming platforms add more ads, apps become heavier, subscriptions multiply, and useful features are often locked behind accounts, tracking, or closed ecosystems. KenjiGames responds to that by building projects that feel more direct.

Open-source work is not only a technical choice. It is a trust choice. When a project can be inspected, people can understand what it does and why. That does not make every project perfect, but it creates a healthier relationship than asking users to accept everything blindly.

A Broader Creative Hub

KenjiGames is still creative at its core. The same energy that started with games now shows up in cinematic interfaces, media tools, research archives, YouTube projects, and utilities for everyday problems. The work can be playful, serious, experimental, or practical, as long as it has a reason to exist.

The long-term goal is to keep expanding without losing the original independence. Build the tools, publish the work, question the defaults, and make projects that people can actually use.

That also means the work does not have to pretend to be neutral about everything. We prefer tools that reduce friction, respect the viewer or listener, and make it easier to step outside systems that are designed mainly around ads, lock-in, or passive consumption.
