What You Can Find Here

KenjiGames is a home for projects that are practical, cinematic, independent, and willing to question the defaults.

Some visitors come here for entertainment software: a better way to stream, listen, download, or organize media. Others come for research and public-interest work. The site brings those directions together because they are connected by the same belief: people should have clearer tools, fewer distractions, and more control over the systems they use.

That is why the projects are not only presented as code repositories. Each page should explain what the project is, who it helps, and why it exists. The technical details still matter, but they should support the human purpose instead of replacing it.

Why We Build

KenjiGames started with gaming, but the mission grew into something broader: tools that give users more control.

Open Media Experiences

IVIDS and IVIDS Music are built around a simple frustration: media apps often put ads, tracking, and platform control before the viewer. Our projects explore cleaner ways to watch, listen, organize, and enjoy content across devices.

Open Source By Default

We prefer projects that can be inspected, learned from, and improved. Open-source work keeps the relationship with users more honest because the code, direction, and tradeoffs are easier to see.

Ethical Curiosity

Not every KenjiGames project is a media player. The Great Awakening shows the other side of the mission: documenting influence, asking hard questions, and building spaces where people can investigate for themselves.

From Games To Independent Tools

KenjiGames started from the same place many creative projects start: experimenting with games, interfaces, videos, and ideas that felt exciting enough to build. That gaming origin still shapes the style of the site. The interfaces are cinematic, fast, and visual because entertainment software should feel alive.

But the purpose has expanded. Today the projects are less about one category and more about independence. Some tools help you watch or listen without the usual advertising pressure. Some help you organize your own files instead of relying on closed libraries. Some, like The Great Awakening, are about research, power, and public accountability.

A More Honest Digital Experience

The common thread is simple: users deserve software that is useful before it is profitable. That means fewer distractions, clearer interfaces, open repositories where possible, and projects that are honest about what they are trying to do.

IVIDS is the clearest example of that media direction. It imagines streaming and Live TV as one clean hub instead of a mess of separate apps and ad-heavy screens. IVIDS Music takes the same idea into listening. Together with the rest of the projects, they show where KenjiGames is going: practical, open, ethical tools with a strong visual identity.
