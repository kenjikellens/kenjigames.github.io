Organizing the Chaos of Modern Media

In the current landscape of anime, keeping track of what you've watched has become a complex task. With franchises spanning multiple TV seasons, OVAs, movies, and spin-offs, traditional "flat list" trackers often fail to capture the true structure of a series. Anime Tracker was created to solve this problem through a sophisticated, hierarchical approach to media management.

Our platform isn't just about checking boxes; it's about understanding the relationships between different media entries. By grouping series into unified "Franchises," we provide users with a bird's-eye view of their progress across an entire cinematic universe, ensuring that nothing is missed and everything is perfectly organized.

Technical Deep Dive

The architecture of Anime Tracker is built on a custom three-layer entity model: **Franchise -> Series Type -> Episode Progress**. This structure allows for a level of organization that is impossible in traditional database schemas, enabling features like automated franchise completion percentages and complex seasonal sorting.

The Complexity of API Synchronization

Synchronizing data with the AniList GraphQL API presented unique challenges, particularly when handling rate limits and data consistency across multiple sessions. Anime Tracker utilizes a robust queuing system that batches updates and handles conflict resolution in real-time, ensuring that your local state and cloud database are always in perfect harmony without the user needing to manually trigger a sync.

The Power Watcher's UI

Every element of the Anime Tracker interface is designed for speed. From the "Batch Update" engine that allows users to mark dozens of episodes as watched in a single click, to the "Seasonal Predictor" that highlights upcoming releases based on your current watchlist, the UI is optimized for power users who manage hundreds of entries.
