Streaming, Live TV, And Your Library Together

Most entertainment setups are fragmented. One app has movies, another has shows, another has live channels, and many of them surround the actual content with ads or distracting recommendations. IVIDS tries to bring those experiences back into one clean player, with the focus on what you want to watch instead of what a platform wants to push.

The goal is a living-room friendly streaming service that can also work as a Live TV player. Browse channels, open video content, continue across screens, and use the same familiar interface on Android TV, mobile, desktop, or a browser. IVIDS is especially useful when you want a unified entertainment hub without the usual advertising-first feeling of modern streaming platforms.

Behind The Player

IVIDS is still built with performance in mind, but the technology serves the viewing experience. The interface is designed for big screens, remotes, touch screens, and browsers, with fast movement between sections and layouts that adapt to the device in front of you.

The open-source codebase makes it easier to inspect, adapt, and improve the player over time. That matters because entertainment software should not have to be a closed black box that only serves the platform owner.

IVIDS can be explored through the repository or run as a lightweight web interface, with Android-oriented packaging for TV and mobile use.
